  1. From Physics and First Principles
  • Fiability

    physics laws are thoroughly validated, well known, and generalizable

  • Explicability

    models are easier to understand, at least at a high level of abstraction


  • Difficulty

    building a model from first principle is hard, not always possible.

  • Slow

    in general case, simulation is computationnally intensive

  1. From Data and Machine Learning
  • Easier

    we let the neural network and the optimization extract patterns from the data

  • Faster

    once trained, they are faster than most simulation algorithms


  • Generalizability

    no guarantee that the model will generalize outside training domain

  • Reliability

    no guarantee of robustness to noise and/or stability

The Output Prediction task

Non-linear Dynamical System: $\quad \left\{\begin{array}{cll} \dot{\mathbf{s}} &= {\color{#277C9D} f}(\mathbf{s}) & \quad\Rightarrow \color{#888888}\small{\textit{ Dynamics}}\\ \mathbf{y} &= {\color{#277C9D} h}(\mathbf{s}) & \quad\Rightarrow \color{#888888}\small\textit{ Output}\\ \end{array}\right.$
How can we design a different dynamical model capable of simulating the observation?
Definition: Output Predictor [Janny et al. 2020]

An output predictor is defined as a couple $G, \psi$ such as:

  • $\dot{z} = G(z, y)$ is a uniform exponential contraction,
  • the couple $(g, \psi)$ with $g(z)= G(z, \psi(z))$ is a generating model.

Deep KKL: Data-driven Output Prediction for Non-Linear Systems

From Non-Linear to Linear

Koopman theory

For all non-linear system $\dot{\mathbf{s}} = {\color{#277C9D} f}(\mathbf{s})$, there exists an infinite dimensional linear operator $\mathcal{K}$ such that $\mathcal{K} \mathbf{h}(\mathbf{s}) = \mathbf{h}({\color{#277C9D} f}(\mathbf{s}))$.

Lusch, B., Kutz, J. N., & Brunton, S. L. (2018). Deep learning for universal linear embeddings of nonlinear dynamics. Nature communications
Kazantzis-Kravaris-Luenberger (KKL) theory

For any non-linear system $\dot{\mathbf{s}} = {\color{#277C9D} f}(\mathbf{s})$ and for any paire $A, b$, there exists an injective mapping $T$ such that $\dot{\mathbf{z}} = A \mathbf{z} + by$ and $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{h}\Big(T^{-1}(y)\Big)$ where $\mathbf{z}$ is of finite dimension.

Andrieu, V., & Praly, L. (2006). On the existence of a Kazantzis--Kravaris/Luenberger observer. SIAM Journal on Control and Optimization

Take home messages

  • Deep KKL is a powerful inductive bias

    • Many practical tasks do not require a full-fledged deep neural network with fancy GNN or Transformers, but rather a simple, fast and robust model capable of simulating the output of a dynamical system.

  • A step toward more principled theory on RNN

    • Deep KKL provides a simpler framework to study the properties of RNNs, by explaining their success using contraction theory.

  • Do not replace your RNNs with Deep KKL !

    • We do not claim that Deep KKL is better than RNNs, but rather that it is a different inductive bias that can be useful in some cases, especially when dealing with dynamical systems.

  • Limitation to "small" systems

    • Deep KKL suffers from a limitation of control theory: it do not scale well with the dimension of the system. Addressing larger-scale problems is (currently) a challenge.

Take home messages

  • First large-scale dataset for CFD

    • The eagle dataset is the first fluid dynamics dataset of such scale, and is publicly available. It is a big leap in difficulty compared to previous datasets, and is a step toward better neural simulators.

  • Mesh Transformer is SOTA on CFD tasks

    • Our Mesh Transformer achieves state-of-the-art results on the eagle dataset, and on classical benchmarks such as Cylinder-flow. It also offers a form of interpretability via attention maps.

  • Limited to 2D simulations

    • Addressing 3D simulation in an accurate and efficient way is still a challenge. For now, Eagle focuses on 2D simulations with an engineering-grade turbulence model. Extending to 3D simulations requires way more data and computational power.

  • Going further with inductive bias

    • The strength of our Mesh Transformer is also its weakness: it differs from conventional solvers by allowing non-local interactions, which improves greatly the accuracy but also makes the use of physical priors more difficult.

Identifiability [Janny et al. 2022]

The experiment $(\mathbf{AB}, \mathbf{CD}, z)$ is identifiable if and only if, for any set of confounders $z'$:

Counterfactuality [Janny et al. 2022]

Let $z^k$ be the set of confounders $z$ where the $k^{th}$ value has been modified. The experiment $(\mathbf{AB}, \mathbf{CD}, z)$ is counterfactual if and only if:

Filtered-CoPhy: Unsupervised Learning of Counterfactual Physics in Pixel Space

Take home messages

  • Dataset for causal discovery in physics

    • The Filtered-Cophy dataset is an improvement over CoPhy, the first dataset for causal discovery in physics, yet eliminating the ill-posedness of the tasks and addressing the more challenging problem of reasoning in pixel space.

  • Object-level representation via keypoints

    • Our keypoints detector is designed to use a limited set of keypoints to describe complex 3d objects using shape coefficients that are discovered via the unsupervised pre-training. This representation is interpretable and can be used for downstream tasks such as counterfactual prediction.

  • Apparent simplicity / True challenge

    • At first glance, the task seems not so complicated. Yet, collisions are a very difficult phenomenon to model, which make the dataset challenging.

  • A lot of opportunity for follow-up work

    • Several parts of the model can benefit from a V2 update to account for new progress in the field.
